Interests

Latent Information Representation in the Brain
One major question in neuroscience is how information is represented within the brain. One promising research direction which may proove fruitfull is Neural Manifolds. Neural Manifolds are low-dimensional structures on which the temporal evolution of higher dimensional neural activity is constrained through the network connectivity. One example to clarify this concept is the neural manifold of the head direction cells. Described in a paper Professor Ila Fietes group used topological data analysis along with nonlinear dimensionality reduction techniques in order to establish that the activity of the head direction circuit lies on a ring manifold. The network has many thousand neurons, and therefore its opulation code has equivalently high dimension (as the population code consists of the vector of instantaneous firing rates of neurons). Nevertheless, the entire activity lies close to a convoluted ring, which is a 1D topological structure. Once a ring manifold had been identified, splines could be fitted in order to determine the position of the population activity along the ring. These splines could then be used to perform unsupervised heading direction estimates by assuming the manifold employs isometric coding, and fitting an initial position to it.
Synergy between Computational Neuroscience and Machine Learining
Trivially machine learning has taken many inspirations from computational neuroscience. Arguably the field is rooted in the invention of the McCulloch-Pitts Neuron which came the collaboration between a neuroscientist and a logician. Currently, we are entering an era where the discoveries and inspirations are not unilateral anymore. This is exemplefied by the paradigm of using Recurrent Neural Networks to mimic neural activity patterns, which then allow for a more holistic analysis of the system. This paradigm is famously applied in the work of Mante et al.. Here they analysed the neural activity of maquace monkeys prefrontal cortices while perfoming a behaivioural task involving visual integration and selection. Here they found that the representation of inputs and the choice the monkey will ultimately make are seperabe in neural statespace. Nevertheless, they were able to train a Recurrent neural network to reproduce the population dynamics, indicating that selection and integration of task-relevant inputs are part of the same underlying dynamical process. Through analyses of the trained RNN, they were also able to indicate the mechanistic dynamical processes which allowed the coexsitance of these two constituents within the same dynamical system.
Bio-plausible Learning Algorithms
One of the major driving forces of the Deep-learning revolution has been the use of the backpropagation algorithm. Rooted in control theory it allows a loss gradient to be calculated in terms of the network parameters. This formulation could then be used with already established gradient descend techniques. Nevertheless, backpropagation proves to be biologically implausible due to a number of factors including the lack of feedback weight plasticity and therefore weight symmetry, the need for distinct phases including forward and backward passed, and the need for global information IE weight transport to occur. To mitigate these biological implausibilities, a number of alternate algorithms have been proposed. These most-prominently include Feedback Alignment and variants of it, Target Propagation and variants, and Local Layer-wise learning. Nevertheless, these algorithms, although coming close, do not match the performance of backpropagation. Furthermore, real neural systems communicate via spikes and therefore will inherently use the spiking information for the weight updates. Although some properties like spike dependant plasticity are known, they do not allow spiking neural networks to effectively navigate a function space.
Personalised Approaches in Medicine
High variability in patient response to treatment is a big problem in medicine. This problem is highly exacerbated by the fact that most reference data was collected from caucasian males which is a big issue in itself. This lead to standard treatments being designed on profoundly biased reference data and therefore variable responses especially across ethnicities and genders are immense. I explored the possibility of personalised approaches in catheter ablation in my Bachelor Thesis. Atrial Fibrilation (AF) is the most common form of sustained cardiac arrythmia and represents a major risk factor for ischemic stroke and severely increases mortality. The only Curative Treatment is Catheter ablation which has a reoccurence rate of over 45% in the first 12 months. One problem with catheter ablation is that it uses the same treatment procedure regardless of the patients atrial geometry and fibrosis topography which are both highly relevant in generating and sustaining AF. Therefore I came up with the idea of using Deep Reinforcement Learning in order to create catheter ablations based on bachelor project variable atrial geometries makes Until complete personalised medical approaches are available for people of every socioeconomic background and ethnicity, I strongly believe there should be more efforts to collect relevant reference data to help reduce patient treatment response variability. complete personalisation is still a distant prospect therefore advocate of new references
