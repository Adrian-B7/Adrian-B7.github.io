Interests

Latent Information Representation in the Brain
One major question in neuroscience is how information is represented within the brain. One promising research direction which may proove fruitfull is Neural Manifolds. Neural Manifolds are low-dimensional structures on which neural computation is constrained through the network connectivity. One example to clarify this concept is the neural manifold of the head direction cells. Described in a paper Professor Ila Fietes group used topological data analysis along with nonlinear dimensionality reduction techniques in order to establish that the activity of the HD circuit lies on a ring manifold. They then used splines fitted to this manifold to perform unsupervised heading direction estimates by assuming the manifold employs isometric codint, and found exceptional agreement.
Synergy between Computational Neuroscience and Machine Learining
Trivially machine learning has taken many inspirations from computational neuroscience. Arguably the field is rooted in the invention of the McCulloch-Pitts Neuron which came the collaboration between a neuroscientist and a logician. Currently, we are entering an era where the discoveries and inspirations are not unilateral anymore. This is exemplefied by the paradigm of using Recurrent Neural Networks to mimic neural activity patterns, which then allow for a more holistic analysis of the system. This paradigm is famously applied in the work of Mante et al., where they fit an RNN to the neural dynamics of maquace monkeys while perfoming a behaivioural task involving visual integration and selection.
